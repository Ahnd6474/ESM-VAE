DMS Performance Analysis

A key challenge in protein engineering is predicting the effects of mutations. [cite_start]ESMS VAE excels in this domain, demonstrating superior performance on Deep Mutational Scanning (DMS) datasets from Protein Gym. [cite: 94]

[cite_start]

When evaluated across 162 datasets, ESMS VAE achieved a mean Spearman's ρ of 0.7779. [cite: 97] [cite_start]This significantly outperforms the supervised model Kermut, which had a mean Spearman constant of 0.6982 on the same datasets. [cite: 100] [cite_start]This high correlation indicates that the model's latent space effectively captures the functional impact of mutational changes. [cite: 8]

Key Capabilities

Reconstruction & Generalization

[cite_start]

Achieved a 97.17% reconstruction rate on a test set randomly sampled from UniRef50, proving its ability to generalize to diverse proteins. [cite: 4, 80]

Novel Protein Generation

[cite_start]

Generated sequences show a maximum identity of only ~10% with training data, confirming the model creates entirely new proteins rather than copies. [cite: 5, 6]

Fluorescent Protein (FP) Analysis

    [cite_start]
  • Classification: 98.7% 5-fold CV accuracy in classifying FPs and non-FPs. [cite: 9, 108]
    • [cite_start]
  • Regression: Low RMSE values of 2.7nm (excitation) and 3.8nm (emission) for wavelength prediction. [cite: 9]

Architecture & Training

[cite_start]

ESMS VAE is a lightweight 5.5M-parameter transformer composed of four encoder and four decoder layers. [cite: 64] [cite_start]Training uses a custom structural loss based on ESMS embeddings so the latent space captures three-dimensional features. [cite: 42, 45] [cite_start]On a UniRef50 subset the model reached 97.17% reconstruction accuracy and remained robust even when noise was added. [cite: 4, 83]
